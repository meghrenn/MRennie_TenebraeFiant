Pessimism

And so it came to pass that the Education Angel declared that God Almighty had decreed that the worthy worshippers in the City to the South would have the chance of choosing where the educational jewel would be located. And it came to pass that the royal bankers and commercial kings and trust managers set their powerful machine in motion to crush the insolent opposition, but they did this secretly through their hired mouthpiece whom year after year they kept as their puppet in the chambers to the north. None could withstand the power of that machine. Futile and frantic fists flayed its sides, but it ground on and on and on until all sheep had been rounded up and counted; and God Almighty proved once again victorious. The perfidious faculty melted away; the student body seeped out of the city; a quiet came, and creeping death. The jewel had a name; it had a site, but its lustre was gone.

The city sagged. Long rows of houses empty stood and stores with vacant windows. Royal bankers, commercial kings and real estate lords sweated in their secret sanctuaries. Where were the funds which should be flowing? Where was the gold which should be growing? God Almighty pondered. What was amiss? Could he have been wrong?"
